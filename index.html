The Brian Compton Stall

Entertainment/Production • $27K tier • Died: Post-proposal

NO CLOSE

THE MOMENT

"How quickly could you get us onboarded?"

→ Rep answered with process details

→ No close attempt

→ "I'll send you info"

THE AUTOPSY

  • • Buying signal ignored
  • • Info given vs commitment asked
  • • No next step scheduled
  • • Momentum died

The Carly K Price Anchor

Music/Studio • $21K tier • Died: Price shock

NO CLOSE

THE MOMENT

"Why is QuickBooks so expensive?" "Dumb questions about..."

→ Price discussed early

→ Shame about knowledge gaps

→ Value not established

THE AUTOPSY

  • • Price came before value
  • • Shame not normalized
  • • Founder time cost not calculated
  • • $21K felt like expense, not savings

THE FIX

Should have done first:

"You mentioned 10 hours/month on finance. At your rate, that's $5K in founder time. We do it for $1,800. You save $3,200 AND get better data."

Transcript Rewrites

What was said vs what should have been said. Exact dialogue.

SCENARIO 1: The Buying Signal

ACTUAL

What Happened (Deal Died)

Prospect: "How quickly could you get us onboarded?"

Rep: "Oh, usually within a week or two. Depends on complexity. We need discovery first, then setup takes 3-5 days..."

Prospect: [listens]

Rep: "I'll send you some info to review."

Result: No next step. Deal stalled.

REWRITE

What Should Happen (Deal Advances)

Prospect: "How quickly could you get us onboarded?"

Rep: "If we could have you fully onboarded by next Monday, would that work for your timeline?"

Prospect: "Yeah, that would actually be perfect."

Rep: "Great. Let's schedule your kickoff call for Thursday. Do mornings or afternoons work better?"

Result: Kickoff scheduled. Deal advanced.

SCENARIO 2: The Price Question

ACTUAL

What Happened (Objection Escalated)

Prospect: "That seems expensive."

Rep: "Well, our pricing is competitive for the market. We offer a lot of value..."

Prospect: [pushes back harder]

Result: Defensive spiral. No close.

REWRITE

What Should Happen (Price Reframed)

Prospect: "That seems expensive."

Rep: "I get it. Let me ask—how many hours are you spending on finance each month?"

Prospect: "Probably 15 hours or so."

Rep: "At your effective rate, that's $7,500 in founder time. Our service is $2,300. You're actually saving $5,200 per month while getting better data. Expensive would be keeping it in-house."

Result: Price objection neutralized. Value established.

SCENARIO 3: The Transition Fear

ACTUAL

What Happened (Fear Won)

Prospect: "What's going to happen to our current bookkeeper?"

Rep: "We would take over the day-to-day operations..."

Prospect: [anxiety visible]

Result: Fear not addressed. Deal lost.

REWRITE

What Should Happen (Fear Calmed)

Prospect: "What's going to happen to our current bookkeeper?"

Rep: "We build a bridge, not a wall. Your bookkeeper works with us for 30 days, learns our systems, and becomes your internal liaison. They level up, you get continuity, nothing falls through the cracks. Want me to walk you through exactly how that handoff works?"

Prospect: "Yes, that would help."

Result: Fear addressed. Continuity established.

Exact Scripts

Word-for-word what works. Copy. Paste. Adapt.

OPENING (Entertainment Industry)

"I know [Carmel/your weekend/etc] was great. Listen, when it rains it pours—you've obviously got momentum building.

I'm curious, how's your current finance setup handling all this growth? Are you still drowning in the month-end close or have you got that sorted?"

Acknowledges success first. Asks about pain. Positions as enabler.

FOUNDER TIME CALCULATOR

"Quick question—how many hours are you spending on finance stuff each month? Ballpark.

[They answer]

And what's your effective hourly rate? If you weren't doing books, what would that time generate?

[They answer $X]

So you're spending $[15 × $X = $Y] in founder time on finance. Our service is $2,300. You're actually saving $[Y-2300] per month AND getting better data."

Makes them say the number. Reframe from cost to savings.

TRIAL CLOSE

"If we could solve [specific pain they mentioned] and have you up and running by [specific date], would that work for your timeline?"

[Wait. Let them answer.]

"Great. Should we schedule the kickoff call for [day]? Do mornings or afternoons work better?"

Specific. Assumptive. Next step locked.

OBJECTION: "Let me think about it"

"Totally get it. Can I ask—what specifically would help you feel confident about moving forward?

[Listen]

Got it. How about this—I send you [specific thing] by [specific time], we chat [specific day] at [specific time], and if it makes sense, we kick off [specific date]. Fair?"

Gets real objection. Specific next steps. No escape hatch.

OBJECTION: "It's expensive"

"I hear you. Let me ask—how many hours are you spending on finance right now?

[They answer]

And if you had those hours back, what would you focus on?

[They answer]

Right. So it's not $2,300—it's $2,300 minus the revenue you'd generate with that time back. Plus the leaks we'd find. Most clients discover $40-60K in annual leaks they didn't know about."

Questions, not defense. Opportunity cost + hidden value.

CREDIBILITY DROP

"You'll work with John—he's our East Coast director, former [Big Four], one of my former colleagues. He's incredibly capable, smart, meticulous, and calm. Exactly what you need when everything's on fire.

Want me to introduce you to him directly?"

Specific person. Behavioral descriptors. Offers connection.
